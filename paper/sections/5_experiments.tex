\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:exp_setup}

\paragraph{Simulation environment.}
We implement an event-driven bus simulation modeling a single bidirectional route with 22 stations (11 per direction), serving 264 scheduled trips per day.
Passenger demand follows origin--destination (OD) matrices with time-varying arrival rates calibrated to reproduce morning and evening peaks.
The simulation advances at a resolution of $\Delta t = 1$\,s.
Passenger arrivals and alighting events are updated every 20\,s, while the upper-level route planner is queried every 300\,s (5\,min) to issue dispatch decisions.
Travel times between consecutive stations are drawn from log-normal distributions whose parameters vary by time of day and direction, with a route stochasticity parameter $\sigma$ controlling variance (default $\sigma = 1.5$).

\paragraph{Methods.}
We compare six variants, labeled \textbf{A}--\textbf{F}:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{\Alph*.},leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{TransitDuet (full)}: the complete framework with TAP, $\thetavec$-OGD, Lagrangian penalties, and cosine-annealed $\beta$ schedule.
    \item \textbf{No TAP} ($\beta = 0$ always): both levels train independently; the cross-advantage augmentation is disabled.
    \item \textbf{No $\thetavec$-OGD} (fixed reward weights): the measurement-projection constraint on fleet utilization is replaced by fixed, hand-tuned reward weights.
    \item \textbf{No Lagrangian} ($\climit = \infty$): the headway-regularity constraint at the lower level is removed; the agent relies on reward shaping alone.
    \item \textbf{Fixed Timetable} ($\piU$ disabled): the upper policy is replaced by a static timetable with $\htarget = 360$\,s throughout the day; only the lower-level holding controller is trained.
    \item \textbf{Flat RL}: a single-level distributional soft actor-critic (DSAC) agent that jointly selects dispatch and holding actions without hierarchical decomposition.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Metrics.}
We report four primary metrics averaged over the final 50 evaluation episodes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Average passenger wait time} (min): the mean time between a passenger's arrival at a station and boarding.
    \item \emph{Bunching rate} (\%): the fraction of station arrivals where the forward headway is less than half the target headway.
    \item \emph{Peak fleet size}: the maximum number of buses simultaneously in service during an episode.
    \item \emph{Episode reward}: the undiscounted cumulative reward over one full day of operation.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Training protocol.}
Each method is trained for 300 episodes with three independent random seeds.
We report means and standard deviations across seeds.

\label{sec:mechanism}

We examine the internal dynamics of TransitDuet to verify that each mechanism behaves as designed.

\Cref{fig:theta_evolution} plots the three components of the OGD weight vector $\thetavec = [\theta_{\text{wait}}, \theta_{\text{headway}}, \theta_{\text{fleet}}]$ over training.
Early in training, $\theta_{\text{fleet}}$ dominates as the policy learns to satisfy the fleet constraint; after approximately episode \textcolor{red}{\textbf{[TBD]}}, $\theta_{\text{wait}}$ and $\theta_{\text{headway}}$ increase as the policy shifts toward service quality optimization within the feasible region.
